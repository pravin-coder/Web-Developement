What is E-Voting?

e-Voting is voting through an electronic system on resolutions of company requiring members/shareholderâ€™s consent. The need for e-Voting arises when a company wishes to pass a resolution by Postal Ballot or Annual General Meeting or Extraordinary General Meeting.

Ministry of Corporate Affairs, Government of India has authorized NSDL for setting up an electronic platform to facilitate members / shareholders to cast vote in electronic form. e-Voting system created by NSDL is certified by Standardization Testing and Quality Certification (STQC) Directorate, Department of Information Technology, Ministry of Communications & IT, Government of India.

In addition to providing remote based evoting option, NSDL also facilitates companies to provide electronic voting option at meeting venue using portable devices like Tablets. Further, NSDL helps companies to conduct virtual meetings and live streaming of meeting proceedings.

E-voting eliminates the need for physical polling stations, long queues, and paper ballots. Instead, voters can securely vote from their computers or mobile devices, making the electoral process more efficient and accommodating to busy lifestyles.

Why Choose E-Voting?

E-voting offers several advantages, including:

  • Convenience: Vote from the comfort of your home or anywhere with an internet connection.
  • Accessibility: It's inclusive for people with disabilities and those who find it challenging to travel to a polling station.
  • Security: Rigorous security measures are in place to protect your vote and personal information.
  • Transparency: Real-time access to election results and historical data for analysis.

Eligibility and Registration:

To participate in the e-voting system, individuals must be eligible voters according to the applicable laws and regulations.

Voters must register with their valid identification, ensuring they meet the age and residency requirements.

Voter Authentication

he e-voting system must incorporate robust authentication measures to verify the identity of voters, such as biometric data, two-factor authentication, or government-issued IDs.

The e-voting system must be accessible to all eligible voters, including those with disabilities. It should comply with accessibility standards..
